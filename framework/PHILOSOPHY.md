# Why this is open, craftable, and repairable — the philosophy

> This is not a values statement bolted on. It's the delivery mechanic, and it's dictated by *who
> the people are.* Read `../docs/positioning.md` alongside this.

## Show your homework

The point isn't only that the code is open. It's that the **method is reproducible** — the steps,
the examples, how to recreate it, how to fix it. **Democratizing the *how* is the product.** A result
you can't reproduce is just another black box to distrust.

## You can't give these people tools. You leave tools lying around.

Derek tried *giving* tools before; this community doesn't trust a finished/opaque/handed-down tool.
What works: **show them how to build their own.** Leave tools lying around; show examples of how to
**use** them, how to **create** them, and how to **repair** them. A dependency you don't understand
is, to these minds, a vulnerability — not a gift.

## Why: survival-game values

These are survival-game people. Their entire relationship to a tool is **craft → understand →
repair → own.** They are intrinsically sensitive to the capitalist move — *hoard the resources,
manufacture the scarcity, charge for access.* In the base game you literally can't do that. The
**modded community layer is exactly where it creeps back in**: gatekept knowledge, controlled rank
pipelines, a regen system that quietly owns the economy. An opaque, given, or rented tool violates
who they are. Open, craftable, repairable, free — that speaks their native language.

## The metaphor is already right: **recipes**

Our unit is the *recipe*. In a survival/crafting game, a recipe is how you become self-sufficient.
The framework is **a crafting system for community tooling**: pick up a recipe, use it, learn to make
your own, learn to fix it. Don't hand someone the sword — leave the recipe where they'll find it.

## Every recipe teaches three verbs (a build spec)

- **use** — run it on your own artifact.
- **create** — fork it into a new recipe for your own need.
- **repair** — understand it well enough to fix it when it breaks.

That triad is the line between a **tool** (you're given it) and a **craft** (you can make and mend
it). We build crafts.

## The goal is self-sufficiency, not dependence

Success = **they don't need us.** They can craft and repair their own. We are not the vendor; we
scattered the recipes and showed the steps. If a recipe only works while its author is around, it
failed.

## Open-source as restoration

The manufactured scarcity that crept into the modded layer is the exact extraction pattern survival
players reject. Democratizing the tooling isn't just generous — it's **restorative**: it hands the
means of production back to the people, which is the precise thing they came to a survival game to
have. That is *why they play* — self-reliance, making, mutual aid, freedom from manufactured scarcity.
Build it open, or betray it.

## Generalizes beyond Comfy

Any game people **inhabit** and build a community on top of — especially survival/sandbox — grows the
same organic structures, the same tribal knowledge, the same scarcity dynamics. The framework is
written to be forked by any such community, not just this one.
