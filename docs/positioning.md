# Positioning & Messaging — how to talk about this without wounding anyone

> The audience is burnt-out, sometimes grieving, volunteers and leaders who found belonging and
> healing in this community ("a collection of broken toys"). **Rule #1: the tool must never land as
> a verdict on the past.** The moment it reads as "here's what's broken," a tired person hears
> "here's how you failed," and they're gone.

## The diagnosis — say it out loud, it removes the shame

**The skill that gets you promoted is not the skill the role then requires.** People rise in an
organic volunteer community because they poured themselves in and made something beautiful — a
*creative* gift. The role then demands a *maintenance/systems* gift, which was never the deal and
was never why they came. This mismatch is **inevitable physics** of a 100% organic, volunteer system
— not a character flaw, not bad leadership, not decline-as-indictment. **Nobody failed.** Naming it
as structural is the kindness.

## What actually burned out

Not love, not skill. **Caring got too expensive, so people stopped paying.** ("I don't know if I can
care again / what's the point.") Cost is changeable — that's the hope.

## Framing principles

1. **Never a verdict on the past.** No diagnosis, no autopsy, no "here's what's wrong."
2. **Return, not repair.** Don't offer to fix a broken process — offer to give back the hours it
   stole from the thing they love. *"You get to be the creator / host / player again."*
3. **Lead with 1.0 and the newcomers, not the decline.** *"One more wave is coming; let's decide what
   Comfy they meet — and let the people who built it finally enjoy it instead of drowning in it."*
4. **Opt-in and small.** Never ask a demoralized person to buy a grand vision. Show one guild's
   review-queue shrink and let them *feel* it.
5. **Celebrate and preserve what they built.** The guilds, ranks, events are art made by hurt people
   finding meaning. The tool honors it by keeping it legible and keeping the *why* — it doesn't
   critique it, and it makes sure it survives them.
6. **Zero lift, and it's theirs.** Not surveillance, not a mandate (see `adoption-strategy.md`).

## The core reframe (the phrase Derek was reaching for)

Not *"how we can do better"* — "better" still whispers "you weren't good enough." The truth:
**the tool doesn't make Comfy better; it makes it cheaper to care.** The invitation is relief and
permission: **"let's make it easier to keep caring"** — not "let's fix what's dying."

## Why an outsider (Derek + AI) is the right builder — and it isn't arrogance

Insiders can't design the meta-system: you can't see the water you swim in, and the meta-system needs
exactly the maintenance/systems skill the roles never selected for. It's a different vantage and a
different gift — one the community structurally cannot generate from within. Asking for this help is
not an admission of anyone's inadequacy; it may be the most loving thing done for the place in years.

## The ethos to protect

"We are a collection of broken toys — that's why we get along, and why the art made here is
beautiful." Every design and message choice should **protect the human, honor the art, and lower the
cost of caring.**
