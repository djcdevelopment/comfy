# Perspective: Ivo — the everyday player, "the best of humanity"

> The lens that gave us the perception gap and the ban/accountability wound. A lens on
> [`../kernel.md`](../kernel.md).

## Who / the lens
An everyday player — happy, social, generous; Derek calls him "the best of humanity." The clearest
window into how the *ground* experiences the place, and into what breaks trust from below.

## Where it entered
Early: "Ivo still plays Comfy… would suggest it to a friend." Later, and more sharply: the ban story —
where the *happiest person* was **sad**, and Derek drilled in.

## What he sees (fears · hopes · why he plays · view of the structure)
- **Why he plays:** it's a *great building server* — low friction to start, no need to enter any race or
  competition to enjoy it.
- **Hope:** to be able to recommend it to a friend without hesitation.
- **The perception gap:** the culture *looks* "sweaty"/competitive, but **most people don't play that
  way** — so casual/builder players can't find each other, and the front door misrepresents the welcome.
- **The ban wound:** the powers-that-be can just ban — *no documentation, no evidence, no recourse.* "If
  there's nothing to hide, why isn't the evidence posted?" One unproven story poisons trust for everyone.

## What we learned through this perspective
- The **perception gap** → a discoverability/matchmaking problem; the guild system is a *latent
  playstyle-sorter* with no visibility. (You can recommend the real Comfy only if people can *find* it.)
- The **frustration-vs-sadness diagnostic:** others' complaints were friction (fixable tooling); *Ivo's
  sadness* signalled a **moral wound** — "this place isn't who I thought it was." Sadness from the
  happiest person is the deepest alarm.
- **Unaccountable bans kill recruitment:** inviting a friend is *vouching*; you can't vouch for an
  authority you can't trust. A friend banned with no backup **breaks the handshake bond** → honest
  people stop recruiting → the healthiest growth vector dies.
- → accountability (documented reasons + posted evidence + real recourse) = the **anti-appreciation**
  removed; the loop made trustworthy.

## How it evolved
"Ivo would recommend it, but it looks sweaty" (the perception gap, first data) → much later, his
*sadness* → the ban/accountability/handshake insight → the **"unaccountable rejection" amplifier** in the
kernel, and the (most politically delicate) ban-accountability recipe.

## What it changed in the kernel / build
The perception-gap insight (playstyle discoverability); the *anti-appreciation* problem amplifier;
the ban-accountability recipe candidate — framed additively ("if there's nothing to hide…").
