# Perspective: Bruce — OG creator, systems-builder, conscience of trust-and-scale

> The oldest lens: a creator from *before* the rules. Loves equations; also a gamer. A lens on
> [`../kernel.md`](../kernel.md).

## Who / the lens
A long-time Creator and OG from before process/rules existed. A brilliant systems-builder who thinks in
equations and mechanics. The community's memory of what it was like *before* the machinery.

## Where it entered
Derek relayed "Bruce's story" (the trust-vs-scale timeline); later asked Bruce directly what would get
him back to playing.

## What he sees (fears · hopes · why he plays · view of the structure)
- **Why he plays:** creative expression and precision building — "how to color every pixel, set the
  angle, the degree"; the Obliterator, harpoon + feather-cap, the "skillet method"; *shenanigans.*
- **Hope:** *"let people build their own."* The old no-mods rule existed to grow the playerbase (mods
  scared people); mods are prolific now — so let guilds build their own integrations, and everyone gets
  better tools and more engagement.
- **Fear / wound:** resignation. "It doesn't matter. I can't get through to upper leadership."
- **Structure view:** era 4 everyone knew everyone → era 5 the lag fix caused a player explosion → era 6
  rules enforced *to scale* → **trust nose-dived.** "Every scale of efficiency removes personalization."
  Automation done right *frees* humans ("prevents players squabbling over loot").

## What we learned through this perspective
- The **trust-vs-scale curve** — the core diagnosis of how the community broke.
- **"Efficiency removes personalization"** → the central design constraint: *buy back the human element;
  never add rigidity.*
- The **officer paradox** and the **condensation of the pillars** (creative sector vs. guild GMs) —
  surfaced around his era; forcing omniscience destroyed healthy specialization.
- **"Let people build their own"** = our whole thesis in his words → the un-basement, user-owned tooling.
- His question **"is there even a feedback loop for the structure?"** → answered by the vanity-metric /
  broken-feedback-loop diagnosis, and by the whole Core aim (close the appreciation loop).

## How it evolved
From "a sad OG story" → the trust-vs-scale diagnosis → the design constraint → the no-basement
architecture (his "let people build their own" made literal) → **Bruce's projection**: the model
rendered as *systems dynamics / equations* — the trust curve, regen↔economy, the vanity-metric loop —
the cheap proof that a systems-builder can turn over in his hands.

## What it changed in the kernel / build
"Absorb the load, protect the human"; no-basement; the officer-paradox + condensation + vanity-metric
amplifiers. His projection is the flagship *rigorous* artifact (systems-dynamics).
